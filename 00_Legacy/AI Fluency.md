AI Fluency - isn't about a technical expert but about developing practical skills, insight, and values that reinforce each other and adapt as technology changes

Engage with AI systems in ways that are Effective, Efficient, Ethical, and Safe
- Really maximizing what you get out of your interaction with AI without wasting time and energy

 AI isn't just a tool, although can act as a tool, but a medium, a partner or co-creator

 ![[Screenshot 2025-08-18 at 06.43.09.png]]
To **DELEGATE** effectively:
- understand your goal and the problem that you are trying to solve
- know what AI systems can and can't do well
- decide how to divide the work between you and the AI
Delegation isn't just about offloading tasks but it is about having a **clear vision** and **strategically choosing how AI fits into your process**.

**DESCRIPTION** - focuses on clear communication with AI
- description goes beyond just writing prompt
- having detailed, context-rich conversation that establish:
	- what you want the final output to be
	- how you want the AI to approach the task
	- how you want the AI to behave

**DISCERNMENT** - thoughtfully evaluating what AI gives you
- are the facts accurate?
- does the reasoning makes sense?
- do the recommendation align with your brand values and audience?
- does this output ***actually*** helps you move forward
Discernment draws upon your own expertise in a domain and requires developing developing the judgment and critical insight to **separate what's useful and what's not** and to recognize when **AI outputs needs refinement or should be set aside entirely**

 **DILIGENCE** - focuses on responsible AI interactions
 - how are you verifying the accuracy of the information presented to you
	 - are you protecting sensitive data
- have you considered how to be transparent about the involvement of AI
- are you willing to be accountable for the AI assisted work you have done


## 4 Ds Dive Deep

### **Delegation**
- the cornerstone of good delegation isn't about AI at all. It's about **your own expertise** and your own **understanding of what you are trying to accomplish**.

##### Problem Awareness - the ability to clearly define your goals and understand what work is needed before involving AI tools

- What does "success" look like?
	- what exactly are you trying to accomplish?
	- what are you trying to create or solve?
	- what is your vision or **key project goals?**
- What kind of thinking and work is needed to get there?
	- are there areas that are simple but time-consuming?
	- are there areas of uncertainty where you could use a trusted thinking partner?
	- are there areas of ignorance where you need more data?
	- are there areas that require critical judgment?
- AI fluency begins with and depends on your expertise
