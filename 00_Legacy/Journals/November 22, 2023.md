# Perfect Asian Fried Rice

Start with rice made at least a few hours before that you dry out in the fridge (or on the countertop if you live in a desert like me). Type of rice doesn't particularly matter unless you pick some weird stuff like basmati or brown rice that's not supposed to become fried rice. Always wash your rice, Chinese style rice is not supposed to be gooey or sticky but rather dry.

Heat wok up to max, add splash of high temp oil and coat inside surface, then immediately throw in your eggs and vigorously scramble/fry with a pinch of salt. Set aside.

Use **thawed** vegetables, stir fry like above w/max heat and generous splash of oil immediately before adding ingredients. Add more durable veggies like carrot before delicate veggies or aromatics like onion or garlic. At max heat it should only need a minute or two of full blast cooking before you add your rice and spices. Fry until texture is satisfactory, then turn off heat and allow it to cool a bit before adding soy sauce. Soy sauce should cook on lower heat because it can easily burn and turn bitter if you're not careful.

You must always be tossing and turning your ingredients over extremely high heat to get that restaurant flavor you're looking for. The key is to always keep heat high and moisture low to avoid boiling your ingredients - this is why you cannot use frozen veggies. *Chinese stir frying is 90% preparation of ingredients*; you do not have time to mess with getting stuff out of the fridge or cutting/peeling ingredients. Get it all organized onto a plate before you start, your final result will reflect this effort.
