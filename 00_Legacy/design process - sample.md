Theory: **the coffee cup inspired architectural design**
(at least in user interfaces)

1. choose the initial element to design
2. design that element according to your *statement* or story
3. choose next element to design, *just* make it suitable for on the previous element/s
4. repeat number 3 until you finish everything

Notes:
- you need to think a lot on the first 2 steps
- the remaining steps should not require you to use a lot of mental energy
 - "The designer _does not_, as a rule, _begin with some preconceived idea_. Rather, the idea is (or should be) the **result of careful study and observation**, and the design a product of that idea."



#### 1. choose the initial element to design
- *start at the epicenter*

#### 2. design that element according to your *statement* or story
- what is the story of the element?
	- ask why
	- ask what if
- if you cannot specify a story: choose a simple, common design
- ALWAYS sketch in paper first (because it's the fastest)
	- then on a design tool to see it digitally
